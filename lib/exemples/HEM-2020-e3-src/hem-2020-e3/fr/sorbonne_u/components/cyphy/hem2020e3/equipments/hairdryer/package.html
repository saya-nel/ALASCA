This package defines a hair dryer component used in the Household Energy Management example. It includes a SIL simulation described in its subpackage fr.sorbonne_u.components.cyphy.hem2020e3.equipments.hairdryer.sil.

The hair dryer component is defined as an uncontrollable appliance in the Household Energy Management example i.e., an appliance that os totally under the control of the user but can't be managed by the house energy manager. A haire dryer can be turned on and off, and when it is on it can be put in a low (less hot and less consuming) or high (hotter and more consuming) mode. The interface HairDryerCI proposes the corresponding services.

The purpose of the Household Energy Management example is to be able to track the electricity consumption of a house/flat in order to cut off some appliances when the production of (green) electricity is insufficient. Hence, the electricity consumption of the appliances must be simulated in order to perform software testing without connection to the actual appliance. Here, SIL simulation is sought, so the simulation models in the sil subpackage are meant to cooperate with the code in the component.

To support this, the service implementations in the HairDryer component will both keep track of the state of the hair dryer (switch on/off, low/high mode) and trigger events in the simulation models to force state changes and the update of the electricity consumption.
